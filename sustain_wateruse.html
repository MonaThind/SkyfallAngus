Water Use

Capture & Store Rainfall

Capturing rainfall is one of the most effective ways to secure water for the future. Well‑designed storage systems help reduce reliance on external or groundwater sources, improve drought preparedness, lower long‑term operating costs, and minimise runoff and erosion. From tanks to strategically placed farm dams, efficient storage is the backbone of a resilient water system.

Use Water‑Efficient Irrigation

Modern irrigation systems — especially drip irrigation — deliver water directly to plant roots, reducing waste and improving crop performance. Benefits include lower evaporation losses, reduced runoff, precise nutrient delivery, and improved water‑use efficiency across the farm. This approach is especially valuable in water‑limited regions.

Design the Landscape to Hold Water

Good land design helps slow, spread, and sink water into the soil. Techniques such as contour banks, swales, riparian buffers, and vegetation strips reduce erosion, improve infiltration, and protect waterways. These features strengthen the landscape’s natural ability to retain moisture.

Build Soil That Holds More Water

Healthy soil acts like a sponge. Practices that improve soil structure — including cover cropping, conservation tillage, organic amendments, and rotational grazing — increase water retention. Better soil means more moisture available for plants, even in dry periods.

Monitor Water Use & Quality

Sustainable water management includes regular monitoring of salinity, pH, turbidity, mineral content, and storage levels. Clean, reliable water supports livestock health, pasture growth, and overall farm performance.

Plan for Drought

A strong drought plan includes assessing stock and domestic water needs, ensuring adequate storage capacity, reticulating water to key areas, and building redundancy into the system. Proactive planning reduces the impact of dry seasons on livestock, pastures, and natural assets.

Protect Water Sources

Healthy catchments produce cleaner, more reliable water. Key practices include fencing off dams and waterways, managing erosion, maintaining vegetation around water sources, and preventing contamination from livestock access. These steps support both productivity and environmental health.
